¿Quién?

Andrew Taylor Still


Osteopathy was founded in the late 1800s by the physician and surgeon Andrew Taylor Still in Kirksville, Missouri. He discovered that in order to achieve the highest possible form of health, all parts of the body should work together harmoniously. His goal was to restore the body to optimum health with minimal surgery and medicine, influenced in part by the realisation that medical treatments of that time were largely ineffective and in some cases, harmful.


By 1892, at the age of 64, Dr. Still began teaching formally. He founded the American School of Osteopathy (now the Kirksville College of Osteopathic Medicine) in Kirksville, Missouri. The first class consisted of only 22 students, both Men and Women.


Today, there are 57 Osteopathic Medical Schools in 33 states of the United States.

¿Cómo?


Dr. Still was a visionary. He believed his students should be complete physicians. He granted his graduates the degree D.O. (Doctor of Osteopathy) and many of them became very skilled in the manual diagnostic and therapeutic skills he called Osteopathy.


A remarkable student of Dr. Still was William Garner Sutherland DO (1873-1954), graduated from the American School of Osteopathy in 1900. He took the admonition to “keep digging” very seriously and revealed “Osteopathy In The Cranial Field”
